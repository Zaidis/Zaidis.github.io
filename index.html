My Work

Anima - Study Abroad Project

Anima was created during a cooperation with UW Whitewater and HAN University in the Netherlands. Sadly due to the pandemic, this project had to become remote over the spring and summer. Nonetheless, I still was able to work along side the HAN students from the Netherlands, and we made good use of Jera to organize our project the best we could.

I was part of one of the two programming teams in Anima. My team focused on the environment mechanics that the player would face. I wrote the code for pressure plates, door opening, and I also helped with the character programming and bug fixing. Sadly the website to this project was closed, but I still have access to the files!

Spring and Summer of 2020

The Watcher - Team Project

The Watcher was created at the beginning of the Covid 19 Pandemic. Originally made in 48 hours during a local game jam, The Watcher was published on itch.io, and over the course of a few days it became the top game under the "popular" tag for the survival genre with thousands of downloads and views with good ratings.

I was the lead map designer for this project, so my largest job was to create the map and decorate it with foliage and random objects. However, I also participated in programming. I created the note system and helped with level transition.

Spring 2020

Lyra's Tale - Personal Project

Lyra's Tale is my own personal RPG I created over the course of around ten months. I have always wanted to make my own "Zelda" game where the player embarks on a journey through various maps, slaying monsters, and having a great adventure.

Every single asset in the game is my own, which includes the art, the story, the programming, and even the music. For the programming, I created my own inventory system, crafting system, input system, combat, dialogue trees, and more. I am very proud of this game even though it is a demo, and I intend to keep working on this.

Spring 2020 - Spring 2021

Aroa - Team Project

Aroa was created during the Brackey's 2020 game jam, which was a week long. The theme was "rewind," and so we decided to make a game about time. We came up with the idea to make a horror game placed in a mansion, where there is a large sum of clocks ticking and spinning.

I was the lead designer for this game and one of the two programmers. I created the key/door system, the riddle puzzle safe in the basement, all of the player mechanics (such as movement, sprinting, picking up objects), and I made the entire map out of the Unity tool: Probuilder. This Project was awarded an "Honorable Mention" in the 2021 Spring MAGD Expo!
